## pixel-sort (2026-05-13)

Pixel-sort (Kim Asendorf ASDF, 2010 — contiguous-run threshold sort along scan lines) — Step 2 patternisation against portrait.jpg. Defaults tuned by sweep: direction=row, sortBy=luminance, thresholdLow=90, thresholdHigh=200 — the [90..200] band sorts the midtones (skin, fabric) while shadow walls (<90) and highlight walls (>200) hold the face together. Wider bands like [60..240] dissolve features; narrower like [120..180] reads as a near no-op. Row beats column/diagonal for portraits because horizontal streaks read most clearly as "sort" rather than smear. Two modes shipped across cycleMs=15000: `band` (thresholdLow ping-pongs 30 ↔ 150 — dark wall slides, sorted region grows/shrinks from the shadow side) and `flow` (thresholdHigh ping-pongs 140 ↔ 250 — bright wall slides at the opposite end of the band, so the breath is highlight-driven).
